Armed Conquest / Boss Guide

Every boss distilled into a readable brief — mechanics, bounties, and how to beat them — so you don't have to scrub through videos. Strategies paraphrased and condensed from community written guides (credited below). CN patch data; adapt to your own roster.

How the mode works

  • Locked pilots: a pilot who clears a battle is locked out for the rest of the section until you've beaten every battle in it — so this mode rewards roster depth, not just a few strong pilots. Only pilots lock; your STs are always reusable.
  • 3 bounties per battle: achievement-style challenges with rewards. Once done they're permanent. You can clear them with your best pilots, then re-run with weaker ones to free the strong ones up.
  • Mercenaries: hired pilots follow the same lock rule — a pilot used once (yours or hired) can't be reused elsewhere in the section until unlocked.
  • Weekly reset (Monday): claim a reward for each completed battle without refighting.
  • Challenge budget: once you've unlocked 4 sections, you can only run 3 sections' worth of challenges per week — pick which weapon materials you want.
  • Rewards: Section 1 gives Lv70 S-class weapons; from Section 2 on, battles drop crafting materials for high-end / unique weapons (several battles per weapon).
  • Difficulty: Section 1 is single-difficulty. From Section 2, Normal → Hard → Extreme unlock as you clear all 4 bosses; Extreme adds a stage-wide effect and tightens bounty requirements.
  • Unlock gates: later sections sit behind Frontier Trial depth % and clearing the previous section's Hard.

Unlock requirements & progression

The mode itself arrived with patch 1.2.1 and sits behind campaign / Frontier Trial progression — you'll be well into the endgame before it opens. Each section then has its own gate (below), and within a section every battle you clear locks the pilots used until you've beaten all battles in it. Note: only pilots lock — your STs are always free to reuse.

Section unlock gates
  • 1 · Trapped Beast — base section, available with the mode. Single difficulty.
  • 2 · Thunder Rumbles — reach 90% depth in Frontier Trial To the Very End and clear Clearance Ops Storm From the Sea in one run (that CO itself needs 50% depth in To the Very End first). Roughly 3–4 weeks of play for the 90%.
  • 3 · Cross The Line — reach 80% depth in Frontier Trial Escape Impossible.
  • 4 · Restless Soul — reach 80% depth in Frontier Trial Neglect AND clear all Hard stages of Cross The Line.
  • 5 · Empty Promise — clear all Hard stages of Restless Soul (plus a release-date gate; shipped with the Evangelion collab).
  • 6 · Longest Night — reach 90% depth in Frontier Trial Dawn Hour AND clear all Hard stages of Empty Promise. Inferno unlocks after all Extreme.
  • 7–8 · Lost in the Desert / Wildfire — later sections following the same pattern (Frontier Trial depth + previous section's Hard clears); exact gates not confirmed here.
Difficulty structure
  • Section 1 is single difficulty.
  • From Section 2, Normal → Hard → Extreme each unlock after you beat all 4 bosses on the tier below.
  • Extreme adds a stage-wide effect and tightens the bounty requirements. Longest Night onward adds an Inferno tier after all Extreme is cleared.
Rewards by section
  • Section 1 drops complete Lv70 S-class weapons.
  • Section 2 onward drops crafting materials for S+ / SS / unique weapons — several battles' worth per weapon.
  • Some sections drop SS backpack blueprint materials (e.g. Longest Night, Lost in the Desert).
  • Each Monday you can claim a reward for every completed battle without refighting. Once 4 sections are unlocked, you're capped at 3 sections' worth of challenge chances per week — pick your materials.

Universal tactics — how to beat most stages

  • Bounty rotation (the efficiency trick): clear a battle's 3 bounties with your best team, then replay it with A-ranks / alternates. The replay locks the weaker pilots and frees your S-tiers again — so save your bombers for the hardest battle in the section.
  • Kill the body, not the boss: most bosses have enormous body HP and self-repair. Break the limbs first, then focus everything on the body the moment it's exposed.
  • Break arms to disable, legs to pin: destroying an arm usually shuts off a boss's skills or halves its output; destroying legs stops movement and flanking on adds.
  • Guts bait: an "immortal" Guardian/Guts pilot (Catherine, Ana) eats the AoE and one-shots and controls where the boss moves and who it targets. Keep it fed AP with a mechanic.
  • Seven + Pochy: Pochy acts as a pseudo 5th unit, a bait for missiles/rockets, and (with her shield unlocked) area repair — invaluable on 4-slot maps.
  • Status = speed: the 6-turn "Quick Resolution" bounty usually needs a status applier — shock (Winston, Cicero) or burning ground (Eileen, Lucia, Erisa) to stack extra damage per turn.
  • Bring the right mechanic: debuff bosses need cleanse (Blazer ST, Melissa); AP-starve fights need Gareth/Lorna to refill your bait or re-attack your carry.
  • Snipers do double duty: extended-range snipers (Natalia, Kelly, Shawnee) hit the body from outside counter range and pick off flanking adds that would otherwise wreck your formation.
  • Manage adds, don't chase them: destroy grunt weapons or legs rather than killing — many respawn, some explode, and several bounties cap how many you may defeat.
  • Spread vs AoE: never stack units against rocket/AoE bosses — spacing both survives the hit and clears the "hit fewer than N units" bounties.
  • Read the Extreme effect first: each Extreme adds a stage-wide rule that usually breaks one of your normal tactics (no keep-away, forced pairing, stealth adds…). Plan around it before you deploy.
